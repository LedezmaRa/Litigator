ⓘ Metrics Guide — what do these scores mean?
Composite Score (0–100)
Weighted blend of trend strength, momentum, volume conviction, and fundamental quality. Higher = more favourable setup overall.
Entry Score (0–100)
Measures how close the stock is to an ideal low-risk entry point right now. Combines EMA proximity, ATR-based risk, and volume confirmation.
RS Rating (0–99)
Relative Strength vs. the S&P 500 over the past 12 months. 99 = top 1% of all stocks. Derived from IBD’s methodology.
ADX (Average Directional Index)
Measures trend strength (not direction). ADX > 25 = trending; > 40 = strong trend. Used to filter out choppy, non-directional markets.
ATR (Average True Range)
Average daily price range over 14 days — a volatility gauge. Used to size positions and set stop-loss distances proportional to the stock’s natural movement.
EMA Stack
Price is above its 20-day EMA, which is above the 50-day, which is above the 200-day. When all three align this way, the stock is in a confirmed uptrend.
Squeeze Score
Measures volatility compression (Bollinger Bands inside Keltner Channels). High squeeze = coiled spring setup; a breakout from squeeze often produces large moves.
Rel 3M
3-month price return relative to SPY (S&P 500 ETF). Positive = outperforming the market over 3 months.
Days to Cover
Short interest divided by average daily volume. How many trading days it would take all short sellers to buy back their positions. High = potential squeeze fuel.
IV Rank
Where today’s implied volatility sits relative to its 52-week range (0–100%). High IV Rank = options are expensive; low = relatively cheap.
